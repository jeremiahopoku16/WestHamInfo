About West Ham United.

West Ham United Football Club, often referred to as "The Hammers", is a professional football club based in East London, England. Founded in 1895, the club has a rich history and a strong following of passionate fans. We're delighted to provide you with comprehensive information about this iconic club. Club History

West Ham United Football Club boasts a captivating and illustrious club history that spans over a century. Founded in 1895 as Thames Ironworks FC, the club quickly became a symbol of East London's industrial spirit. Renamed West Ham United in 1900, the Hammers have navigated the ebbs and flows of English football, leaving an indelible mark on the sport. From their early years in the Southern League to their promotion to the Football League First Division in 1923, West Ham's journey has been one of perseverance and passion. Notably, their victory in the 1964 FA Cup, often referred to as the "Boys of '64," remains a cherished moment in the club's history. Over the years, the club has showcased brilliant talents, including the iconic Bobby Moore, Geoff Hurst, and Martin Peters. Today, West Ham United continues to add to its rich legacy with a committed fanbase and a presence in the Premier League, solidifying its place as a beloved and historic institution in English football.
